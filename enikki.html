Playing in the Sunflower Field

Boku’s vacation diary

Hands down the most charming thing about the original Bokunatsu (and subsequent entries in the series), the game’s save screen takes the form of a composition book Boku is filling with pictures and narrations of his daily experiences, most likely to share with his class once he’s back in school. Each day, the protagonist will comment on a single highlight, be it receiving a special item, meeting someone new, visiting a beautiful area, or even getting a bad dream. Conversely, a lack of interesting events can also be reflected in this diary, and failing to make Boku’s summer memorable will eventually lock players out of any of the possible post-ending secret scenes, which have the character, now an adult, thinking back to the many ways his vacation shaped up his life.
