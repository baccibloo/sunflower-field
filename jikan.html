Playing in the Sunflower Field

The passing of time as a gameplay mechanic

From its very premise, Boku no Natsuyasumi is a game that focuses on the passing of time, which not only helps ground the game in reality but also demands a small degree of strategy from players. With only so many hours available each day (and with Boku’s uncle restricting players from straying far away at nighttime), going through the game requires some planning and memorization in order to ensure you visit the places and people you want to see each day. In addition to this, as the month goes by, Boku will run into obstacles that require players to both find out the right tools to sort them out and to also take several days to work around said obstacles. This adds a deeper sense of natural progression to the game, with some of these little discoveries, like the makeshift bridges that lead to new areas, feeling more satisfying after seeing the main character put his effort into a task for several days.
